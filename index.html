What is this?

Nassim Taleb, in The Black Swan, used two hypothetical cities to make a point - mediocristan and extremistan. In mediocristan, wealth is not scalable (follows a normal distribution), so the difference between the richest few persons and the middle class is relatively insignifant. So much so that if you were to take an average wealth of the population, you could reasonably expect it to accurately represent the average person's income. In extremistan, however, wealth follows a power law distribution - so the richest 1% are significantly more wealthy than the other 99%. If you were to take an average wealth of extremistan, you would not get a reasonably accurate representation of each person's wealth since the 1% skew it so much. This is a visual representation of "extremistan" (which introduces 1% of super-rich) and "mediocristan". However, the wealth is represented as weight!
