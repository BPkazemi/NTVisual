What is this?

In The Black Swan, Nassim Taleb describes two societies, mediocristan and extremistan.

In Mediocristan, wealth is distributed normally, with extremes being highly unlikely. I've represented Mediocristan as Denmark, which has a relatively low Gini coefficient (G=0.24).
In Extremistan, wealth is distributed more like a power-law, with the top percentiles holding the vast majority of the wealth. I've represented Extremistan as South Africa, which has a relatively high Gini coefficient (G=0.631).

The key is that wealth ownership is represented as weight. That is, each circle represents a person, and the corresponding number their weight (aka 'wealth ownership'). Toggle between the two and see the difference!

Note: The visualization essentially represents a Lorenz curve, which means a circle's radius represents not absolute wealth ownership, but relative wealth ownership. Thus, the visualization can only highlight wealth inequality, but gives no insight into absolute wealth. Watch the average to get a sense of the population's inequality.

More details: Everything is being rendered client-side. Check out the source and mess with the Gini coefficients!
